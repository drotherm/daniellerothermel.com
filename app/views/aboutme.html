About Me

I’m from Maryland and for my high school years we lived next to a horse farm which smelled awful but also meant that I got to volunteer there and every once in a while get a free riding lesson. I guess everything in life has tradeoffs.

I’m a Super Senior (aka. in my 9th Semester) at Brown. I started out Computer Engineering but quickly realized that it isn’t the mixture of Physics and Computer Science that I was hoping for. Circuits diagrams are not for me.

I consider David J. Griffiths my honorary friend because I’ve spent so much time reading his Electricity and Magnetism and Quantum Mechanics textbooks while pursuing my Engineering Physics degree.

I've loved Computer Science ever since taking my first CS class freshman year. Each semester I’ve found myself either programming or missing programming.

My first internship was at the New York Times when I finally decided to pursue Software Development this last summer. I worked on the Messaging Team and I loved it. You can read about my experience here.

I look forward to solving challenging problems after I graduate in December. I see myself finding an incredible job in technology where I get to solve exciting problems, possibly exciting problems surrounding Big Data or Machine Learning.

I want to get a PhD after my time in industry. I plan to go back to school to get my PhD in some field of Computer Science, likely Machine Learning. From there, who knows?
